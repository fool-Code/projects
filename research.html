Electron Microscopy

I enjoy working with electron microscopy because it lets us look very closely at our materials. We can not only take detailed images, but also learn about their structure and composition. This helps us understand why they behave in certain ways and how we can improve them.

Python applied to materials science

In materials science, we often rely on paid software to analyse and plot data, and this can create a gap for researchers who need more flexible tools. I believe Python can fill much of this space. With its open-source libraries and machine learning models, we can work on tasks such as image processing, noise reduction, prediction, and many other applications. It gives us more freedom to explore our data and develop our own methods.
